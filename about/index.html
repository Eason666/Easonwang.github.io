About me

I'm what's called a 'Graduate Developer' at the BBC, on their Software Engineering Graduate Scheme. I'm based in MediaCityUK, Manchester and live in the area, but I'm originally from Chester. I recently graduated from Aberystwyth University studying Computer Science.

My main fields of interest are Web standards, Web applications, server-side development, user experience, front-end development and Disney Parks! I've been using the Open Web Platform since the age of 10; back when 2004 when it wasn't so open! I love the change and the silliness of Web standards at the W3C and WHATWG as they are developed.

I contribute frequently to Mozilla. I run nightly builds of Firefox and report bugs and contribute feedback in discussions for new features. Occasionally, I'll also contribute to discussions at the W3C, such as when I proposed a defer attribute in the img element, which was considered for standardisation by the Web Performance Working Group in the Resource Priorities specification. (But it never happened. The standards process takes time, and the Web moved on.)

I'm also a Christian, and a member of Upton Baptist Church.
